Consent Form

Please read the following information carefully before deciding whether to participate in this experiment.

Vicente Malave, a graduate student at the University of California San Diego (UCSD), and his colleagues are conducting a research study to find out more about how humans calculate how similar or how close pairs of objects are. This research is being conducted using the Human Intelligence Task (HIT) on Amazon’s Mechanical Turk (MTurk) service. You are being invited to participate in this study because you are at least 18 years of age, and an MTurk user. We will collect data from about 500 participants.

If you decide to participate in this study, you will be asked to complete a survey in which you compare some objects and indicate how similar they are. For example you may be asked to indicate which cities are closer to each other, how similar animals are to each other, or how similar pictures of faces are. After this you will be asked to complete optional questions providing information about you (age, race, sex, etc.) that will help us analyze the data from the research. Your participation in this research could last from five minutes to one hour, and the expected length per HIT will be indicated in the Mechanical Turk interface.

We believe there are no known risks associated with this research study; however, as with any online related activity, the risk of a breach of confidentiality is always possible. To the best of our ability your answers in this study will remain confidential. We will minimize any risks to breach of confidentiality by keeping your responses completely separate from your worker ID, and researchers will not have access to your worker ID. Research records will be kept confidential to the extent allowed by law and may be reviewed by the UCSD Institutional Review Board.

There is no direct benefit to you from this research. Results from this study will be used to better understand how similarity works. Being able to predict what things people think is the goal of search engines, and the researchers will use the understanding of similarity gained from this research to design better systems.

Participation in this research is entirely voluntary. You may refuse to participate, refuse to answer specific questions, or withdraw at any time without penalty or loss of benefits to which you are entitled. If you decide that you no longer wish to continue in this study, you can simply choose not submit the HIT.

In compensation for your time you will be provided a $3.00 payment by Amazon.com.

If you have questions about this project, or if you have a research-related problem, you may contact the researcher Vicente Malave at 619-800-4113. If you have any questions concerning your rights as a research subject, you may contact the UCSD Human Research Protections Program Office at (858) 657-5100.
